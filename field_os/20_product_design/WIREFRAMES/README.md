# WIREFRAMES

## What lives here

**Wireframes** — low-fidelity structure and layout for the moments defined in experience cards: what appears on a surface, in what hierarchy, in what sequence. Structure only. Not final visual design, not color exploration, not brand work.

- **Purpose.** Give experience cards a concrete, low-fidelity structure that can be assembled and tested.
- **Inputs.** `EXPERIENCE_CARDS/`, `DESIGN_SYSTEM_INTEGRATION.md` (reused patterns and spacing), `MODULE_MAP.md` (module boundaries).
- **Outputs.** Wireframe files per card/flow; each cites its source card. These feed `PROTOTYPES/`.
- **Dependencies.** Accepted experience cards; the existing design system.
- **Responsible role.** Product Designer.
- **Relationship with the approved architecture.** Downstream. Wireframes lay out approved content within approved module boundaries. They keep Field OS, the Recipe & Formulation Engine, and the Knowledge System visibly separate; they never merge modules or expose complexity the architecture hides.
